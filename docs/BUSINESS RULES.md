# ReguFlow AI

## Business Rules Specification

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the business rules governing ReguFlow AI.

Business rules describe:

* Allowed behavior
* Restricted behavior
* Compliance workflows
* Task lifecycle rules
* Audit readiness calculations
* User interaction constraints

These rules must be followed regardless of implementation.

Business Rules take precedence over implementation decisions.

---

# BR-001

Regulation is the Primary Entity

Every workflow in ReguFlow AI must begin with a regulation.

Sources may include:

* RBI
* SEBI
* NPCI
* CERT-In
* Internal Policy
* Uploaded Documents

Nothing can enter the compliance workflow without being associated with a regulation.

---

# BR-002

Every Regulation Must Have a Source

A regulation cannot exist without a source.

Required fields:

* Source Name
* Source Type
* Publication Date

Examples:

* RBI Circular
* SEBI Advisory
* CERT-In Notification

---

# BR-003

Regulations Cannot Be Deleted

Regulations are immutable records.

Allowed:

* Archive
* Supersede
* Mark Inactive

Not Allowed:

* Permanent deletion

Reason:

Audit traceability.

---

# BR-004

Every Regulation Must Have a Risk Level

Allowed values:

* Low
* Medium
* High
* Critical

Risk level may be:

* Assigned manually
* Generated by AI

A regulation cannot remain unclassified.

---

# BR-005

Change Detection Requires Two Versions

Change Detection can only occur when:

Version A exists
AND
Version B exists

Outputs:

* Added Clauses
* Removed Clauses
* Modified Clauses

If only one version exists:

No comparison can be performed.

---

# BR-006

Every Detected Change Must Have Severity

Allowed values:

* Low
* Medium
* High
* Critical

Severity determines prioritization.

Severity must never be empty.

---

# BR-007

Every Change Must Be Explainable

Every detected change must include:

* Change Type
* Description
* Impact Explanation

Users must always understand:

What changed
Why it matters

---

# BR-008

Impact Analysis Must Map to Departments

Every impact analysis must identify at least one department.

Allowed departments:

* Compliance
* Legal
* Operations
* IT
* Cybersecurity
* Audit

Optional:

* Risk
* Finance
* HR

Impact cannot remain department-agnostic.

---

# BR-009

Department Impact Requires Priority

Allowed values:

* Low
* Medium
* High
* Critical

Each department impact must have priority assigned.

---

# BR-010

Every MAP Must Have an Owner

MAP

(Measurable Action Point)

cannot exist without ownership.

Required:

* Owner
* Department

Invalid:

Unassigned MAPs

---

# BR-011

Every MAP Must Have a Due Date

Required:

* Creation Date
* Due Date

Due Date must be later than Creation Date.

---

# BR-012

Every MAP Must Have Status

Allowed statuses:

* Pending
* Assigned
* In Progress
* Review
* Completed

No custom statuses allowed.

---

# BR-013

MAP Status Flow Is Sequential

Allowed transitions:

Pending
→ Assigned

Assigned
→ In Progress

In Progress
→ Review

Review
→ Completed

Disallowed:

Pending
→ Completed

Assigned
→ Completed

Skipping workflow stages is not permitted.

---

# BR-014

Completed MAPs Are Locked

Once status becomes:

Completed

Users may:

* View
* Audit

Users may not:

* Modify
* Delete

Unless reopened by an administrator.

---

# BR-015

Every MAP Must Reference a Regulation

MAPs cannot exist independently.

Required:

* Regulation ID

Each MAP must trace back to a regulation.

---

# BR-016

Audit Readiness Is Based on Completion

Audit readiness is derived from:

Completed MAPs
÷
Total MAPs

Formula:

Audit Readiness =
(Completed MAPs / Total MAPs) × 100

Range:

0–100

---

# BR-017

Audit Readiness Cannot Exceed 100

Maximum:

100

Minimum:

0

Invalid values are prohibited.

---

# BR-018

Findings Affect Audit Readiness

Open findings reduce readiness.

Critical findings reduce readiness more than minor findings.

Critical findings must always be highlighted.

---

# BR-019

Evidence Is Optional For MVP

Hackathon MVP:

Evidence tracking may be simulated.

Future versions:

Evidence becomes mandatory.

---

# BR-020

Reports Are Generated From Existing Data

Reports must never create new compliance records.

Reports are read-only outputs.

---

# BR-021

Executive Reports Use Aggregated Data

Executive reports must summarize:

* Regulations
* Risks
* MAPs
* Audit readiness

Executive reports should not display low-level implementation details.

---

# BR-022

Adaptive Compliance Mode Does Not Change Data

Adaptive modes affect:

* Layout
* Guidance
* Productivity tools

Adaptive modes do NOT affect:

* Regulations
* Risks
* MAPs
* Reports

Business data must remain identical across modes.

---

# BR-023

Beginner Mode Prioritizes Guidance

Beginner Mode must display:

* Tooltips
* Recommendations
* Explanations

Goal:

Reduce onboarding complexity.

---

# BR-024

Expert Mode Prioritizes Productivity

Expert Mode must display:

* Dense tables
* Bulk actions
* Advanced filtering

Goal:

Reduce interaction time.

---

# BR-025

AI Copilot Is Advisory

AI Copilot may:

* Explain
* Summarize
* Recommend

AI Copilot may NOT:

* Override business rules
* Change MAP status
* Modify regulations automatically

Human review remains required.

---

# BR-026

AI Outputs Must Be Traceable

Every AI-generated output should reference:

* Source Regulation
* Clause
* Confidence Score

Traceability is mandatory.

---

# BR-027

Dashboard Metrics Are Read-Only

Dashboard metrics represent system state.

Users cannot directly edit KPI values.

---

# BR-028

Audit Logs Are Immutable

Audit logs:

* Can be viewed
* Can be filtered

Cannot be edited.

Cannot be deleted.

---

# BR-029

Reports Must Preserve Historical Context

Generated reports must reflect the state of the system at generation time.

Reports should not retroactively change.

---

# BR-030

ReguFlow AI Follows Regulation → Action → Proof

Every workflow must support:

Regulation
→ Change Detection
→ Impact Analysis
→ MAP Generation
→ Completion
→ Audit Readiness

This principle governs the entire platform.

No feature should violate this workflow.
